### Summary (Written at the Beginning)

A one-day flash trip is a test of both physical and mental endurance. I planned to rest while waiting for the flight or on the plane, but the waiting time was too early to feel sleepy. On the plane, the seat was too small, I didn’t get a window seat, and the engine noise was loud, so I didn’t really sleep. This meant I was basically sleepless all night. After landing at 6:00 AM, I started the Nagoya itinerary right away. I was so tired that I ended up napping for over half an hour in a quiet, nearly empty café at Nagoya Tower around noon.

Time and attractions are limited, so traveling too far is not feasible.

Besides the body's energy, the phone's battery is also a big challenge; I used a 20,000mAh Xiaomi power bank to complete the entire trip (charging my iPhone 13 about 4–5 times round trip).

Returning to Taiwan around 2–3 AM, there is no public transportation available, so you can only take a taxi back to Taipei.

You can pay a few hundred more to choose a window seat and bring a neck pillow and earplugs for better sleep.

#### 9/11 AM 00:12 — Wandering Around Terminal 1

![](/assets/7b8a0563c157/1*tT0HjtgmHi38ys5yK6j3TQ.jpeg)

It is worth mentioning that Terminal 1 has a free lounge area open to the public. Just follow the signs to the lounge; the environment and seating are similar to a café. There is also a shower room (open from 6 AM to 10 PM). For more details, refer to [this article](https://stancy.tw/airport-lounge/){:target="_blank"}.

The rest area was actually better for sleeping since you could lie down... but I only glanced at it and then started looking for food places in the airport (no airplane meal). As expected, it was too late and everything was closed. In the end, I only found a vending machine selling snacks, so I bought a pack of I-Mei puffs and a can of tea to take with me.

#### 9/11 AM 00:45 — Waiting at the boarding gate

> If I had to choose again, I would pay a few hundred more to get a window seat; first, it’s easier to sleep with something to lean your head on, and second, you can see the sunrise from the window when arriving in Japan in the morning!
