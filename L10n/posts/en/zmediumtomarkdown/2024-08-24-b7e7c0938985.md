#### Memories of Bangkok

Back in 2018, my first company trip after starting work—and my first time traveling abroad—was a 5-day trip to Bangkok and Hua Hin, Thailand. The following year, 2019, I joined the company trip to Sabah. Then came the two years lost to the pandemic. After it ended, I started going crazy over solo trips to Japan.

At that time, I was a total newbie with zero social experience. I blindly followed my older brother and sister without knowing what could or couldn’t be brought on the plane. During security check, I foolishly put my passport on top of the tray and almost freaked out when I saw it fall off the conveyor belt (if it had slipped into the gap, I wouldn’t have gotten it back). That trip was a mindless group tour with bus rides, so my impression of Bangkok was vague—just remembering the great rooftop bar, hot weather, cheap stuff, and affordable massages. I had no idea about the geography at all.

So I always wanted to revisit Bangkok to refresh my memories from six years ago; however, not being familiar with Southeast Asia, I was hesitant to travel alone. **At the beginning of the year, while chatting with Pinkoi colleagues, we decided to organize this five-day free trip to Bangkok.**

Price: `TWD $9,259` (includes additional round-trip **15 kg** checked baggage)

At first, I thought about traveling with my friends. My friend didn’t check in luggage (ticket price was around `$6,000`). Later, I checked other airlines and felt I missed out; regular airlines charge around `$10,000` for similar times. The price difference isn’t big, but the comfort is much better!

Thai VietJet Air allows you to carry two pieces of hand luggage with a total weight not exceeding 7 kg, so checked baggage is not mandatory.

```

- 1 piece of carry-on luggage with maximum dimensions of 56cm x 36cm x 23cm
- 1 small carry-on bag (including the following bags)

  + 1 coat bag with dimensions not exceeding 114cm x 60cm x 11cm when opened.
```

### ⚠️Travel Safety and Precautions in Bangkok, Thailand⚠️

The most important thing when going out is safety. Here is a summary of safety tips and precautions.

#### Scam Prevention / Avoiding Pitfalls

<iframe class="embed-video" loading="lazy" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/o42r-bg16Fg" title="泰國旅行最常見的八種騙局，有些至今仍有人中招！" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen ></iframe>


Based on the summary from Bangkok Cat's video, I actually encountered the money exchange/scam this time.

1. Miscalculating the bill  
   It’s not always intentional; you can calculate the total amount when ordering.

2. Approaching you first to show your country’s currency or ask about exchanging money,  
   then stealing, pickpocketing, or snatching when you take out your wallet.

3. Outside the Grand Palace or other attractions, scammers falsely claim the site is closed and kindly suggest you take a tuk-tuk to other places (Black Temple/black shops/**private boat tours on black boats—encountered this at the pier this time**) to trick you into spending money.

4. Tuk-tuk, shouting prices everywhere, suddenly raising the fare halfway  
   If you want to try tuk-tuks but fear being overcharged, [you can try the new MUVMI app tuk-tuk booking App](https://bkk.com.tw/muvmi-app/){:target="_blank"}  
   Even if the tuk-tuk looks good, beware of scams 3, such as overly eager drivers offering to take you to attractions or boat rides.

5. Fake staff: While waiting in line, fake staff may enthusiastically invite you inside.  
   They will introduce the dishes in your language, help you order, then ask for payment upfront and run away with the money.

6. Motorbike Rental Scams  
   Never leave your passport as a deposit, never leave your passport as a deposit, never leave your passport as a deposit.  
   If you encounter malicious claims for damages requiring compensation, leaving your passport will cause trouble.  
   Choose large chain rental shops and always record a video of the vehicle condition before renting.

7. Local Dating App Scams  
   You might encounter transgender individuals, extortion schemes, or scammers.

8. Patpong Ping Pong Show Scam: On the street, enthusiastic touts ask if you want to see a ping pong show, saying you only need to order a 100 THB drink. Then they lead you into a dark alley or a second-floor black shop.  
   The show is very bad, and when you want to leave, they demand 3,000 THB, claiming the 100 THB drink deal never existed.

9. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places, even at ticket entry points (e.g., Grand Palace, Wat Pho).
