#### Memories of Bangkok

Let's rewind to 2018, my first job's employee trip, and my first time traveling abroad, which was to Bangkok, Thailand (5 days); the following year in 2019, I went to Sabah with the company again; then came the two years stolen by the pandemic, and after it ended, I went crazy with trips to Japan.

Back then, I was a complete novice with zero social experience, blindly following my older siblings on outings. I didn't even know what I could or couldn't bring on a plane. At security, I foolishly placed my passport in the basket, only to realize it had fallen onto the conveyor belt when I exited the machine, nearly giving me a heart attack (if it had fallen into a gap, I wouldn't have been able to return). That trip was a mindless group tour, and my impressions of Bangkok were not very clear; I only remembered that the rooftop bars were great, the weather was hot, things were cheap, and massages were affordable‚Äînothing more, and I had no concept of the geography.

Thus, I always thought about returning to Bangkok to revisit old memories from six years ago. However, being unfamiliar with Southeast Asia, I was hesitant to travel alone. **It just so happened that at the beginning of the year, while chatting with colleagues from Pinkoi, we decided to plan this five-day free trip to Bangkok.**

> _The details and fragments of memories from the 2018 Bangkok + Hua Xin five-day trip are included in the appendix at the end of this article._

Each passenger (except infants) can bring 1 piece of their own luggage and 1 small hand luggage, with a maximum total weight not exceeding 7 kg.

2. Hand luggage dimensions:
- 01 piece with maximum dimensions of 56cm x 36cm x 23cm.
- 01 small hand luggage (including the following bags):
  + 01 bag for girls, magazines, cameras, bags for baby food, and bags purchased at the airport not exceeding 30cm x 20cm x 10cm.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety and Precautions for Traveling in Bangkok, Thailand ‚ö†Ô∏è

The most important thing when going out is safety, so I've compiled a list of safety tips and precautions.
#### Scam Prevention

[![The Eight Most Common Scams When Traveling in Thailand, Some Still Catching Victims Today!](/assets/b7e7c0938985/07b5_hqdefault.jpg "The Eight Most Common Scams When Traveling in Thailand, Some Still Catching Victims Today!")](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o42r-bg16Fg){:target="_blank"}

The following summary is based on a video by the Bangkok Cat. I actually encountered a currency exchange scam this time.
1. Incorrect calculations
   It may not be intentional; you can calculate the total amount when ordering.
2. Someone approaches you wanting to see your country's currency or asking if you want to exchange money.
   When you pull out your wallet, they might steal or pickpocket you.
3. Outside the Grand Palace or attractions, they falsely claim the site is closed and enthusiastically suggest taking a tuk-tuk to other places \(black temples/black shops/ **private boat rides, I encountered this at the pier**\) to scam you into spending.
4. Tuk-tuks often have inflated prices and may suddenly increase the fare halfway.
   If you want to experience a tuk-tuk but are afraid of being scammed, [you can try the new MUVMI app for tuk-tuk rides](https://bkk.com.tw/muvmi-app/){:target="_blank"}.
   Even with seemingly good tuk-tuks, be wary of scam #3, where they enthusiastically suggest attractions or boat rides.
5. Fake staff may enthusiastically lure you into their establishment while you're waiting in line.
   They will introduce the menu in your language, help you order, and then say you need to pay upfront, taking your money and running.
6. Motorcycle and scooter rental scams
   Do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit.
   If you encounter malicious scams claiming damage and asking for compensation, leaving your passport can lead to trouble.
   It's better to rent from reputable chain rental companies, and always take a video of the vehicle's condition before renting.
7. Local dating app scams
   You might end up meeting a ladyboy, a scammer, or worse.
8. The Patpong ping pong show scam, where enthusiastic touts ask if you want to see a ping pong show, claiming you only need to buy a 100 THB drink, then take you into a dark alley or upstairs to a shady establishment.
   It's hard to leave, and when you try, they demand 3,000 THB, claiming the 100 THB deal doesn't exist.
9. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places, even in areas where you need to buy tickets to enter \(e.g., Grand Palace, Wat Pho\).
