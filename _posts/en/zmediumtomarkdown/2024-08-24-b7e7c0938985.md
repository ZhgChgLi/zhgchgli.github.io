#### Memories of Bangkok

Let's rewind to 2018, my first job and my first company trip, which was also my first time traveling abroad, to Bangkok + Hua Hin (5 days); the following year in 2019, I went to Sabah with my colleagues; then came the two years stolen by the pandemic, after which I went crazy with trips to Japan.

Back then, I was a total newbie with zero social experience, blindly following my older siblings around. I didn't even know what I could or couldn't bring on the plane. At security, I foolishly left my passport in the basket, only to find it on the conveyor belt when I exited the machine, nearly giving me a heart attack (if it had fallen into a gap, I wouldn't have been able to return). That trip was a mindless group tour, and my impressions of Bangkok were not very clear; I only remembered that the rooftop bars were great, the weather was hot, things were cheap, and massages were affordableâ€”nothing more, and I had no concept of geography.

Thus, I always thought about returning to Bangkok to relive the memories from six years ago. However, I wasn't familiar with Southeast Asia and was hesitant to travel alone. **It just so happened that at the beginning of the year, while chatting with colleagues from Pinkoi, we decided to plan this five-day free trip to Bangkok.**

#### Scam Prevention

[![The Eight Most Common Scams When Traveling in Thailand, Some Still Catching People!](/assets/b7e7c0938985/07b5_hqdefault.jpg "The Eight Most Common Scams When Traveling in Thailand, Some Still Catching People!")](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o42r-bg16Fg){:target="_blank"}

The following summary is based on a video by Bangkok Cat. I personally encountered a currency exchange scam during this trip.
1. Incorrect calculations
Not necessarily intentional; you can calculate the total amount when ordering.
2. Someone approaches you wanting to see your country's currency or asking if you want to exchange money
They might steal or pickpocket when you take out your wallet.
3. Outside the Grand Palace or attractions, someone falsely claims the site is closed and enthusiastically suggests taking a tuk-tuk to other places \(black temples/black shops/ **private boat rides, I encountered this at the pier** \) to scam you into spending.
4. Tuk-tuks, outrageous prices, suddenly increasing fares halfway through
If you want to experience a tuk-tuk but are afraid of being overcharged, [you can try the new MUVMI app for tuk-tuk rides](https://bkk.com.tw/muvmi-app/){:target="_blank"}.
Even if a tuk-tuk looks good, be wary of scam 3, where they enthusiastically suggest attractions or boat rides.
5. Fake staff members will enthusiastically lure you in while waiting in line, using your language to describe the menu and take your order, then say you need to pay upfront and run off with your money.
6. Motorcycle or scooter rental scams
Do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit.
If you encounter malicious scams claiming damage and asking for compensation, leaving your passport can be troublesome. It's better to rent from reputable chain rental companies and take a video of the vehicle's condition before renting.
7. Local dating app scams
You might end up meeting a ladyboy, a scammer, or worse.
8. Patpong ping pong show scams, where enthusiastic touts ask if you want to see a ping pong show, claiming you only need to buy a 100 THB drink, then lead you into a dark alley or a shady second-floor establishment.
It's hard to leave, and when you want to go, they demand 3,000 THB, claiming there's no such thing as a 100 THB drink.
9. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places, even in areas where you need to buy tickets to enter \(e.g., Grand Palace, Wat Pho\).
