### âš ï¸ Safety Tips and Precautions for Traveling in Bangkok âš ï¸

The most important thing when going out is safety, so I've compiled some safety tips and precautions here.
#### Scam Prevention

[![Common scams in Thailand that travelers often fall for](/assets/b7e7c0938985/07b5_hqdefault.jpg "Common scams in Thailand that travelers often fall for")](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o42r-bg16Fg){:target="_blank"}

The following points are summarized from a video by a Bangkok travel expert. I personally encountered a currency exchange scam during this trip.
1. Incorrect calculations
   It may not always be intentional; you can calculate the total amount when ordering.
2. Someone approaches you asking to see your country's currency or if you want to exchange money
   When you pull out your wallet, they may steal or snatch it.
3. Outside the Grand Palace or other attractions, someone may falsely claim the site is closed and enthusiastically suggest taking a tuk-tuk to other places (like black temples or shops, **I encountered this at the pier**).
4. Tuk-tuks may have inflated prices or suddenly increase the fare halfway through the ride.
   If you want to experience a tuk-tuk but are worried about being overcharged, [try the new MUVMI app for tuk-tuk rides](https://bkk.com.tw/muvmi-app/){:target="_blank"}.
   Even with seemingly reputable tuk-tuks, be cautious of scam #3, where they enthusiastically suggest visiting attractions or taking a boat ride.
5. Fake staff may try to lure you into their restaurant while you're waiting in line, speaking your language to explain the menu and then asking for payment upfront before disappearing with your money.
6. Motorcycle rental scams
   Never leave your passport as a deposit. If you encounter malicious claims of damage, it can become problematic.
   Opt for larger, reputable rental companies, and always record the vehicle's condition before renting.
7. Dating app scams
   You might end up meeting a ladyboy, a scammer, or worse.
8. The Patpong ping pong show scam, where enthusiastic touts ask if you want to see a ping pong show, claiming you only need to buy a drink for THB 100, then take you into a dark alley or a shady establishment.
   It's hard to leave, and when you do, they might demand THB 3,000, claiming the THB 100 deal doesn't exist.
9. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places, even in areas where you need to buy tickets to enter (e.g., the Grand Palace, Wat Pho).

#### Other Tips
- Thailand is not a tipping culture, so generally, you don't need to tip; I only give THB 20 as a small gesture for massages.
- Alcohol can only be sold from 11:00 AM to 2:00 PM and from 5:00 PM to midnight. It's prohibited all day on Buddhist holidays.
