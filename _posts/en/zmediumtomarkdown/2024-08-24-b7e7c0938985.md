#### Memories of Bangkok

Let's rewind to 2018, when I went on my first company trip after entering the workforce, and my first trip abroad was to Bangkok, Thailand (5 days). The following year, in 2019, I joined another company trip to Sabah. Then came the two years stolen by the pandemic, and after it ended, I went crazy with trips to Japan.

Back then, I was a total newbie with zero social experience, blindly following my older siblings on outings. I didn't even know what I could or couldn't bring on a plane. At security, I clumsily placed my passport in the basket, only to realize it had fallen onto the conveyor belt when I exited the machine. I almost had a heart attack (if it had fallen into a gap, I wouldn't have been able to come back). That trip was a mindless group tour, and I don't have a clear impression of Bangkok. I only remember that the rooftop bars were amazing, the weather was hot, everything was cheap, and massages were affordable‚Äînothing more, and I had no concept of the geography.

Thus, I always thought about returning to Bangkok to relive the memories from six years ago. However, I wasn't familiar with Southeast Asia and was hesitant to travel alone. **It just so happened that at the beginning of the year, while chatting with colleagues from Pinkoi, we decided to plan this five-day free trip to Bangkok.**

Thai VietJet Air's luggage policy allows you not to check in luggage; you can carry two pieces of hand luggage, with a total weight not exceeding 7 kg.
```
1. Hand luggage weight:
Each passenger (except infants) can bring 1 piece of their own luggage and 1 small hand luggage, with a maximum total weight not exceeding 7 kg.

2. Hand luggage dimensions:
- 01 piece of hand luggage with maximum dimensions of 56cm x 36cm x 23cm
- 01 piece of small hand luggage (including the following bags)
  + 01 bag for girls, magazines, cameras, baby food bags, and bags purchased at the airport not exceeding 30cm x 20cm x 10cm.
  + 01 bag for coats that does not exceed 114cm x 60cm x 11cm when opened.
```

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety and Precautions for Traveling in Bangkok, Thailand ‚ö†Ô∏è

The most important thing when going out is safety, so here‚Äôs a summary of safety tips and precautions.
#### Scam Prevention

[![The Eight Most Common Scams in Thailand, Some Still Catching Victims!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o42r-bg16Fg){:target="_blank"}](/assets/b7e7c0938985/07b5_hqdefault.jpg)

The following points are summarized from a video by the Bangkok Cat. I actually encountered a currency exchange scam this time.
1. Incorrect calculations
   It may not always be intentional; you can calculate the total amount when ordering.
2. Someone approaches you wanting to see your country's currency or asking to exchange money
   When you pull out your wallet, they might steal or snatch it.
3. Outside the Grand Palace or attractions, they falsely claim the site is closed and enthusiastically suggest taking a tuk-tuk to other places (black temples/black shops/ **private boat rides, I encountered this at the pier this time**).
4. Tuk-tuks, with sky-high prices, suddenly increasing halfway through the ride.
   If you want to experience a tuk-tuk but are afraid of being overcharged, [you can try the new MUVMI app for tuk-tuk rides](https://bkk.com.tw/muvmi-app/){:target="_blank"}.
   Even if a tuk-tuk looks good, be wary of scam 3, where they enthusiastically direct you to attractions or boat rides.
5. Fake staff, while waiting in line, fake employees may enthusiastically lure you inside.
   They will introduce dishes in your language, help you order, and then say you need to pay upfront, taking your money and running.
6. Motorcycle rental scams
   Do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit, do not leave your passport as a deposit.
   If you encounter malicious attempts to charge you for damages, leaving your passport can be troublesome.
   It's better to find a reputable rental company, and always take a video of the vehicle's condition before renting.
7. Local dating app scams
   You might end up meeting a ladyboy, a scammer, or worse.
8. Patpong ping pong show scams, where enthusiastic touts ask if you want to see a ping pong show, claiming you only need to order a drink for THB 100, then take you into a dark alley or a shady second-floor establishment.
   It's hard to leave, and when you want to go, they demand THB 3,000, claiming there's no such thing as a THB 100 drink.
9. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places, even in areas where you need to buy tickets to enter (e.g., Grand Palace, Wat Pho).
