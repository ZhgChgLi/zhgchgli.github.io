### Background of Purchase

Originally, I was just a simple iPhone user. Last year, I got a MacBook Pro and an [Apple Watch S4](../a2920e33e73e/), and I started to get immersed in the Apple ecosystem (commonly known as the Apple family bundle). Having bought the watch, I was just missing a pair of headphones.

The Bluetooth headset I was using had served me for a while; it was a decent headsetâ€”neither bad nor particularly outstanding, with average sound quality and sufficient battery life. If I had to point out its shortcomings, it would be that the call quality was unclear, the signal was easily interfered with, and it required a long press to turn on and off, with unclear pairing and battery indicators. These were all minor issues. I usually used it for commuting and exercising, while I mostly used speakers or wired headphones in front of the computer, so it met my basic needs.

After the release of AirPods 1, most of my friends had positive experiences with them. This time, I happened to catch the trend of AirPods 2, so I decided to get them.

_p.s. Since I had never used the first generation, my considerations for purchasing were not based on comparisons with the first generation, and this article will not mention the differences with the first generation._

### Choosing Between Wireless and Wired Versions

The price difference between the wireless and wired versions is $1,200. Initially, I was considering buying the wireless version; thinking about the messy charging cables on my nightstand and how I could travel with one less cable made it quite tempting for convenience!

After Apple announced that AirPower was dead, I searched online for similar products and bought a 2-in-1 wireless charging version, which could charge both the iPhone and Apple Watch. Since the iPhone and AirPods don't usually need to be charged at the same time, it could be used as a 3-in-1.

Everything seemed great until I placed the order and received the product. In actual use, I found that I couldn't charge both the phone and the watch simultaneously; the watch's charging level was almost zero, and the speed was very slowâ€”the current just couldn't keep up! Even using a 5.1V/2.1A adapter didn't help. I checked [user reviews](https://www.facebook.com/gusha.tw/posts/1219673421523621?comment_id=1255505397940423){:target="_blank"}, and this situation was not unique. In the end, I reluctantly returned it.

Thinking about it, it was just two cables (both the AirPods and iPhone use lightning, while the Apple Watch uses a dedicated cable), and the speed is still faster with wired connections. The wireless version would require that version + the cable + possibly a larger adapter? Compared to that, there was no particular convenience advantage.

**So in the end, I chose the wired version of AirPods 2.**

> p.s. The only difference between the wireless and wired versions is that the charging case for the wired version is the same as the first generation (with the indicator light inside); the wireless version has the indicator light outside and can also be charged with a wired connection.

### User Experience


![](/assets/33afa0ae557d/1*vlDbOyPlOGtttbTXf-Q_IA.jpeg)


Before sharing my experience, let me mention a recent entrepreneurial story I heard; in short, it is: "When making a product, we should not target a broad range, but rather choose a small niche and then gradually expand."

The biggest difference between AirPods and other brands of Bluetooth headphones is the attention to small details in the experience. For example, when you take one earbud out, the music automatically pauses, and when you put it back in, it resumes playback. Plus, you can use them right out of the case without worrying about turning them on or off or connecting them. In terms of comfort, you can hardly feel their presence when wearing them.

The charging speed is incredibly fast, and they automatically charge when placed in the case; so you just need to pay a little attention to whether the case still has power (the case can charge about five times), and you won't encounter the issue of needing to use Bluetooth headphones only to find they are out of battery and having to wait for them to charge.

The latency is as rumored; when watching videos or playing games, you can hardly feel any delay (I tested it with a racing game).

**As for Hey Siri**, I initially thought it was a bit unnecessary since I have a watch that can also activate Hey Siri from a distance; however, after experiencing it, as mentioned above, everything is about "the details." The Hey Siri feature on AirPods takes it to another level, allowing you to call Hey Siri without even raising your hand; you can just call Hey Siri directly, achieving a truly omnipresent feeling with Siri. 

A possible scenario is when you are tidying up around the house with both hands full; at this time, this feature is quite convenient! Also, you can **call Siri to adjust the volume**: "Hey Siri! Turn it up," "Hey Siri! Set the volume to 75%."

To summarize my experience using AirPods in one sentence:


> "Everything feels so natural."


You don't need to spend energy on unnecessary things; headphones should just be headphones.

**The call quality** is equally impressive; in addition to stable basic call quality, the pickup quality rivals that of a microphone, which is truly amazing. During a test call with a friend, he could hardly tell I was using AirPods!

**As for wearing them while riding a bike**, I was initially looking forward to using them for navigation while riding, but a friend who has the first generation said, "No way." With more than 3/4 of a helmet, the process of putting on the helmet presses against the ears, making it easy for the earbuds to fall out. I tested this as well and felt a bit nervous; I recommend that if you need to listen to navigation while riding, just wear one earbud, as it is safer to focus on one ear when putting on and taking off the helmet.
#### **Cons:**

Ultimately, I still need to mention the drawbacks I perceive.

The gestures for controlling functions are too few... I am really used to controlling the volume with gestures (but fortunately, I can control Spotify volume with my watch).

Additionally, while the connection speed to the phone is indeed very fast, the connection speed to the computer is slower: my MacBook Pro 2018 is quite slow, but another Mac Mini connects as quickly as the phone.

The TESTV review channel also mentioned that when using the MacBook Pro with the lid closed and connected externally, the signal with AirPods would be intermittent! (I don't have this issue).

However, why are there these differences? I guess it is due to other signal interference (lights, screen output, other Bluetooth devices), right?

### Anti-fraud Awareness

Please be especially careful, as counterfeit versions with cracked chips have appeared across the strait, pairing with the same animations and battery displays, and are almost indistinguishable from the genuine ones.

The main identification methods currently focus on software:
1. Battery display: Genuine ones can show the battery levels for the left ear, right ear, and case / counterfeit only shows one.
2. In Bluetooth settings, genuine ones can set click functions for the left and right ears / counterfeit only has disconnect and forget options.
3. The charging case indicator light on genuine ones will turn off after connecting / counterfeit will remain lit.

However, it is uncertain whether counterfeit versions will fix these issues in the future, so it's safer to purchase from official or major retail channels.
